Redneck Hot Tub Controller - Help

To protect the hot during freezing weather, be you sure to set the appropriate temperatures in the control panel. The temperature settings for when the Spa heater initially turns on should be set to a temperature that allows the heater to prevent water from freezing. This can be determined based on the water volume of of the spa, the water volume being moved by the Spa pump, and the temperature the hot water being introduced to the Spa from the hot water inlet.

The temperature settings for when the Spa heater turns off when not in use should be set to a temperature above freezing, and the Spa heater can maintain keeping the water below freezing. It might seem obvious, but we do not recommend setting your spa’s temperature at 85°F (29°C) or turning the heater off during the winter if you live in a cold climate. Also setting the energy savings will be offset by the potential cost of replacing burst pipes and damaged hardware.

When using the hot tub enable a setpoint and select the temperature to be maintained during usage. Even though this application can maintain the water temperature, keep a separate thermometer handy or floating in the hot tub. You’ll be able to quickly and easily confirm your spa’s temperature.

Hot Tub Guidelines

The hot tub temperature should be set to not exceed the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommended temperature of 104° F (40° C).

The CDC strongly recommends that kids under the age of five stay out of hot tubs.

Older children should be limited to soak time of five minutes or less, and avoid full immersion. Use a timer to make sure they don’t stay in the water longer than they should.
